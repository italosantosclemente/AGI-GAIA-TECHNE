## 0. Version Note

v0.2 was a transitional design sketch. It introduced the basic idea of the Chirimuuta Haptic Kernel (CHK) but was not committed as a canonical implementation. v0.3 is the first canonical repository version. It incorporates the philological corpus map, the Kant-Cassirer judgment layer, and the historical-genealogical risk detectors.

## 1. Architectural Position

The CHK operates as a critical filter between the formalization of the `Logos` and the normative judgment of the `Ethos`.

`Mythos → Logos → Chirimuuta Haptic Kernel → Ethos`

- **Logos** formalizes and models intelligence.
- **CHK** registers the haptic, material, historical, and experimental cost of that formalization.
- **Ethos** judges whether the resulting claim remains a *Werk* (work) and avoids the attribution of *Wille* (will).

## 2. Core Constraints

The kernel enforces a Kantian-haptic discipline on all AGI claims:

1. **Haptic Traceability:** Every model must be traceable to the material and experimental practices that stabilized its target.
2. **Abstraction Cost Registration:** Simplifications must be made explicit; model success does not license ontological identity.
3. **Anti-Literalism:** computational models of the brain or mind are analogies and idealizations, not literal descriptions.
4. **Judgment Gap:** Rule-following and performance do not equal understanding or judgment (*Urteilskraft*).
5. **Medium Dependence:** The materiality of the substrate matters; substrate-independence is a substantive claim that must be argued, not assumed.

## 3. Claim Evaluation Logic

The kernel classifies claims into a hierarchy of risk:

- **REGULATIVE_HYPOTHESIS:** Valid use of AGI as a heuristic (als ob).
- **ABSTRACTION_RISK:** Formalization that risks losing its haptic anchor.
- **JUDGMENT_GAP:** Equating rule-execution with judgment.
- **CONSTITUTIVE_OVERREACH:** Treating a useful model as an ontological determination of intelligence.
- **WILLE_VIOLATION:** Attributing legislative autonomy or moral agency to the machine.
