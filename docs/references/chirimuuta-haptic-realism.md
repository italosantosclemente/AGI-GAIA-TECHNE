# Chirimuuta Haptic Kernel (CHK) — v0.3 Canonical

For the canonical terminology and triad definitions, see the [Canonical Architecture Map](/docs/references/canonical-architecture-map.md).

The Chirimuuta Haptic Kernel (CHK) serves as the anti-literalization layer for AGI-GAIA-TECHNE. It ensures that every abstraction registers its cost and that computational models are not converted into ontologies of the mind.

## Core Thesis

1. **No abstraction without cost:** Every formalization suppresses particularity; this cost must be explicitly acknowledged.
2. **No model literalized as mind:** The brain-computer relation is an analogy, not a literal identity.
3. **No haptic model converted into ontology:** Models are active, intervention-bound tools (haptic), not descriptions of things-in-themselves.

## Haptic Humility

The CHK measures "Haptic Humility" — the degree to which a claim acknowledges its status as a model rather than a constitutive truth. Claims that fail this check are flagged for **Constitutive Overreach**.

## Risk Detectors

The kernel identifies historical-genealogical risks:
- **Reflex Atomism:** Neglecting organismic complexity in favor of simple I/O chains.
- **Machine-Organism Analogy:** Literalizing the machine model as the truth of the living system.
- **Cartesian Idealization:** Presupposing a clean, self-contained boundary between mind and world.
- **Medium Dependence:** Presupposing substrate independence without argument.
- **Heraclitean Plasticity:** Neglecting the temporal and biological flux of the brain.
- **Prediction/Understanding Split:** Mistaking scale and benchmark performance for genuine intelligence or agency.
- **Technocratic Authority:** Converting scientific abstraction into illegitimate political governance.
- **Apocalyptic Technology:** Treating AI escalation as eschatology rather than technical development.

## Relationship with CTK

The CHK is integrated into the **Clemente Thesis Kernel (CTK)** as a specialized audit layer. While CTK governs the overall architectonic prism, CHK provides the specific haptic "guard" against the literalization of formal models.
