# Individuation: From *Bild* to *Ideal*

## Overview

This document formalizes the structure of individuation within AGI-GAIA-TECHNE, grounding it in Kant's system of transcendental ideas and Bishop's critical analysis of Jung's appropriation of Kant. The individuation documented in *O despertar da minha Razão* (2021–2026, 284pp) is the subjective genesis of the dissertation's objective architecture. No personal biographical details are included — only the philosophical structure.

---

## 1. The Kantian Progression

Kant notes that the progression from self-cognition to world-cognition to cognition of the original being is "so natural that this progression appears similar to the logical advance of reason from premises to a conclusion" (KrV A 334 / B 392).

### Analytic Order (Order of Investigation)
"Proceeding from what experience makes immediately available to us from the doctrine of the soul, to the doctrine of the world and from there all the way to the cognition of God" (KrV A 335 / B 395, note).

This is the order of individuation — ascending from the conditioned to the unconditioned.

### Synthetic Order (Order of Exposition)
The dissertation proceeds in the inverse direction — descending from the unconditioned (the metatheory) to the conditioned (particular determinations of intelligence).

The individuation is the *ascent*; the dissertation is the *descent*.

## 3. The *Bild* → *Ideal* Passage

### Jung's Confusion (Bishop 1996)

Jung treats the *Bild* (primordial image, archetype) as a **constitutive** a priori determinant of psychic life. But for Kant, the Ideas of reason are **regulative**: they orient without determining.

"The image has the advantage of vitality over the Idea" (GW 6, §773) — Jung grants the Image priority over the Idea in terms of vitality. The result: the individual *submits* to the archetype rather than orienting herself toward it.

### The Cassirerian Resolution

| Concept | Jung | Cassirer | Kant |
|---|---|---|---|
| *Bild* (Image) | Constitutive psychic force | *Ausdrucksfunktion* — mythic-affective layer | — |
| *Idee* (Idea) | Abstract, lacks vitality | *Bedeutungsfunktion* — conceptual abstraction | Regulative principle |
| *Ideal* | — (absent) | — (implicit in *focus imaginarius*) | *Urbild* — archetype for complete determination (A 568 / B 596) |
| Mediation | Missing | *Darstellungsfunktion* — presentation | Reflecting judgment |

The individuation is the passage from *Bild* to *Ideal*:
- From the mythic image (expressive, affectively overwhelming, constitutively experienced)
- Through the presentational mediation (the dissertation as *Werk*)
- To the practical archetype (the metatheory as regulative orientation)

### ISC as Transcendental Ideal

Within the LEF, ISC occupies the position of the 25th glyph — the *Princípio Arquitetônico*. Its function: the transcendental ideal in Kant's precise sense.

"As the Idea gives the rule, so the Ideal in such a case serves as the archetype for the complete determination of the copy" (KrV A 569 / B 597).

ISC names the point at which the lived and the legislated converge without collapsing: the author as *intellectus ectypus* who legislates through *Werke*, through symbols, through confrontation with other finite perspectives.

## 5. Individuation as *Bildung*

The individuation is complete not because the Self has been realized, but because the *focus imaginarius* has been recognized for what it is: a regulative ideal that orients without being reached, that makes the journey necessary without making the destination attainable.

The *Despertar da Razão* is not the attainment of Reason, but the awakening to its regulative vocation.

### The Kantian Asymmetry (KrV A 336 / B 393)

"Pure reason has no other aim than the absolute totality of synthesis on the side of conditions" — the ideas "serve only for ascending in the series of conditions to the unconditioned."

The *descent* requires no transcendental use — "for the understanding by itself makes every step downwards from the condition to the conditioned."

- **Individuation** = the ascent (from conditioned to unconditioned)
- **Dissertation** = the descent (from unconditioned to conditioned)
- **AGI-GAIA-TECHNE** = the repository where ascent and descent meet in *Auseinandersetzung*

## 6. The House on the Plain

"We had building materials for a tower that was supposed to reach to the heavens, but the supply was only enough for a dwelling house, spacious enough and high enough for our business on the plain of experience" (KrV B 735).

The individuation is the recognition that the tower was never the point. The modest house — the metatheory, the repository, the *Werke* — is spacious enough for the infinite task.

The hero's journey is neither:
- A Hegelian *Bildungsroman* culminating in Absolute Spirit
- A Jungian individuation ending in constitutive realization of the Self as *Imago Dei*

It is the Kantian-Cassirerian *Bildungsprozess*: infinite formation of the *intellectus ectypus* who knows that the unconditioned can be thought but never cognized, who inhabits the plain of experience in the knowledge that the tower to the heavens was never the point.
